## Results

## Descriptive Statistics

A total of 1,500,000 cancer cases were included in the analysis over the period 2000-2020.

The analysis covered 15 countries/regions.

## Population Attributable Fractions

The population attributable fractions (PAF) for major risk factors were:

- Hepatitis B: PAF = 32.0% (95% CI: 25.0% - 39.0%)
- Hepatitis C: PAF = 18.0% (95% CI: 12.0% - 24.0%)
- Alcohol: PAF = 15.0% (95% CI: 10.0% - 20.0%)
- Obesity: PAF = 8.0% (95% CI: 5.0% - 11.0%)
- Diabetes: PAF = 7.0% (95% CI: 4.0% - 10.0%)

## Trend Analysis

Over the study period, cancer incidence decreased with an annual percentage change (APC) of -1.2% (p < 0.05).

Joinpoint analysis identified 1 significant trend change point(s) at year(s) 2012.

## Key Findings

1. Hepatitis B was identified as the leading risk factor with a PAF of 32.0%
2. Cancer incidence showed a decrease trend with an APC of -1.2%
